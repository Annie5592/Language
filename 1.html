Korean

Korean (South Korean: 한국어/韓國語, hangugeo; North Korean: 조선말/朝鮮말, chosŏnmal) is an East Asian language spoken by about 77 million people,[a] mainly Korean, as of 2010.[2] It is the official and national language of both North Korea and South Korea (originally Korea), with different standardized official forms used in each country.
